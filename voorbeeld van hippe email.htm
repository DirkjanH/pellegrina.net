Dr J. Nefertiti

The Swatch collection called Nefertiti was released in the summer of 1986 and consisted of 3 watches. You’d expect a series with the name Nefertiti to be designed with this queen or her time and contemporaries in mind, but confusingly, this was not the case.

Nefertiti (also: Nefertete or Nofretete) was a queen in the 18th dynasty who ruled Egypt from 1352 – 1338 B.C. beside her husband, the pharaoh Achnaton. The design of these three Swatches however has nothing to do with Nefertiti.

The Swatch Osiris (GM102) was named after a god from Egyptian mythology. The son of Geb – god of the earth – and his sister Noet, goddess of the heavens. According to the myth, Osiris came down from heaven to rule Egypt as the first pharaoh. Osiris was killed by his jealous brother Seth, who chopped his body into pieces and scattered them all over Egypt. Osiris was not just the god of royalty, he was also the personification of the land’s fertility and the spirit of the crop cycle. As ruler of the underworld he granted immortality to those who lived pure lives. Osiris was a very popular god in ancient Egypt. In all of the pharaoh’s tombs pictures of Osiris can be found representing the pharaoh ‘s belief in resurrection.

The Swatch Horus (LL101) was named after the falcon god Horus, son of Osiris and his sister Isis. Because Isis feared their brother Seth would kill her son, like he killed Osiris, she put the baby in a papyrus basket and set it afloat in the Nile, hoping the child would be found and raised to adulthood. After Osiris’ death and the disappearance of his heir, Seth ruled Egypt as a cruel dictator. Upon adulthood, Horus found Seth and demanded his rightful place on the throne. After a long battle he defeated his uncle and became the next pharaoh.

The third watch in the Nefertiti series is called Cosmic Encounter (GS102) and you’ve guessed, it has nothing to do with ancient Egypt.. .Let’s turn our attention to Comet 1P/Halley (Halley’s Comet), probably the best-known and brightest of all short-period comets. The comet is named after Edmond Hally who was the first person, after examining historical records, to determine that the orbital elements of a second comet which had appeared in 1682 were nearly the same as those of two comets which had appeared in 1531. Halley thus concluded that all three comets were in fact the same object returning every 76 years and he predicted its return for 1758. The next apparition of the comet is expected to be in 2061.

I still remember the comet passing in 1986, but it is a mystery to me why this watch and the other two were released in the same series. I know Swatch always incorporates a message in all of its watch designs, but to this day this combination really puzzles me…
